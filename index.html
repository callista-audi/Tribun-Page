B.J. Habibie

The father of Indonesian technology

Here's a time line of B.J. Habibie's life:

  • 1936 - BJ Habibie was born on June 25, in Parepare, South Sulawesi, Indonesia.
  • 1956 - Habibie graduated from the Bandung Institute of Technology with a degree in mechanical engineering.
  • 1956-1962 - He worked as a research assistant at the university.
  • 1962-1964 - Habibie received a scholarship to study aerospace engineering at the Technical University of Aachen in Germany.
  • 1964-1974 - Habibie worked as a research assistant at the university, and earned a doctorate in aerospace engineering in 1974.
  • 1975 - Habibie returned to Indonesia and became involved in the country's aerospace industry.
  • 1978-1998 - He served as Minister of Research and Technology under President Suharto.
  • 1998 - Habibie succeeded Suharto as President of Indonesia following Suharto's resignation on May 21,1998.
  • 1999 - Habibie's presidency ended in 1999, when he was defeated in the presidential election by Abdurrahman Wahid on October 20.
  • 2019 - BJ Habibie passed away on September 11, 2019 at the age of 83.

"Dr. Habibie is a visionary leader who has made significant contributions to the development of Indonesia's science and technology sector."

-- Bambang Brodjonegoro, Minister of National Development Planning of Indonesia
